The Italian Blue Team

The Blue Team (Italian: "Squadra azzurra") represented Italy in international contract bridge tournaments, winning sixteen world titles from 1957 through 1975. From 1964 to 1969 and during a 1972 comeback, the team comprised three regular pairs: Walter Avarelli-Giorgio Belladonna, Pietro Forquet-Benito Garozzo, and Massimo D'Alelio-Camillo Pabis Ticci. Eugenio Chiaradia and Guglielmo Siniscalco played in early years; Dano De Falco, Arturo Franco, and Vito Pittalà in late years. The spiritual father, long-time coach, and non-playing captain through 1966 was Carl'Alberto Perroux.

History

Italy won its first European championship in Open teams in 1951, but lost to the United States in the second Bermuda Bowl. However, members of the Italian team, including Chiaradia, Forquet, and Siniscalco, continued to prepare for future championships. The United States was considered the best team in the world at that time, having won two more Bermuda Bowls against Europe. Great Britain, France, and Sweden were also strong competitors. But in 1956, the Italian Blue Team defeated Bermuda Bowl champion France, earning the right to compete in the 1957 Bermuda Bowl in New York City. The Blue Team went on to defeat a United States team on its home ground, and this victory marked the beginning of a remarkable period of success. With a stable lineup consisting of Walter Avarelli, Giorgio Belladonna, Eugenio Chiaradia, Massimo d'Alelio, Pietro Forquet, and Guglielmo Siniscalco, the Blue Team won twelve world team championships between 1957 and 1969, with only one sixth-place finish. The team retired after 1969, but some key members returned to win four more world titles from 1972 to 1975. The Blue Team's success was largely due to their innovative bidding systems, which were sometimes considered strange or even unfair by conservative US circles. Losing teams complained that the Italians had an advantage using bids that were incomprehensible to Americans. The Blue Team's reign ended in 1976 when the US and Brazil won the Bermuda Bowl and Olympiad, respectively.

Giorgio Belladonna

  • Born: June 7, 1923, Italy
  • Died: May 12, 1995, Rome, Italy
  • wikipedia

One of the best bridge players of all time was Italian. With the Blue Team, he won 16 world championships while playing alongside Walter Avarelli from 1956 to 1969 and Benito Garozzo thereafter. A renowned theorist, he was responsible for developing the Roman Club bidding system starting in 1956. With Benito Garozzo, he developed Super Precision, a sophisticated strong club-based strategy, after 1969. Consider the Belladonna coup for an illustration of how he was renowned for both his brilliant card play and his unpredictable temperament. According to his daughter, Belladonna passed away in Rome on May 12, 1995, from lung cancer.

Walter Avarelli

  • Born: June 3, 1912, Rome, Italy
  • Died: 1987, Rome, Italy
  • wikipedia

An Italian bridge player, a member of the famous Blue Team, with whom he won nine Bermuda Bowls and three World Team Olympiads from 1956 to 1972. Avarelli was born in Rome and became a judge there. He first took up rudimentary bridge during World War II when more than thirty years old. Along with interests in tennis, riding and motor racing, he was especially noted for "his addiction to gastronomy". Avarelli was the longtime partner of Giorgio Belladonna using the Roman Club bidding system. They improved the system together and presented it in a book that was published in at least two Italian editions, 1958 and 1969.

Pietro Forquet

  • Born: 1925, Naples, Italy
  • Died: January 27, 2023
  • wikipedia

An Italian bridge player who was a member of the Blue Team, a highly successful bridge team that dominated international competitions in the 1950s and 1960s. He played alongside Giorgio Belladonna, another legendary bridge player, from 1956 to 1969. During this time, the Blue Team won 16 world championships. After Belladonna's retirement, Avarelli continued to play with the team and won several more championships. He was known for his solid and reliable play style, which complemented Belladonna's more unpredictable approach. Avarelli passed away on June 30, 2010, at the age of 86.

Benito Garozzo

  • Born: 1927 (age 95 years)
  • wikipedia

A bridge player from Italy in America. He played in regular partnerships with Pietro Forquet until 1972, and subsequently with Giorgio Belladonna, winning 13 world championships with the Italian Blue Team, beginning in 1961 when he was added as a last-minute replacement for the Bermuda Bowl. He gained the reputation of being the best bridge player in the world during those championship years, according to many experts.
